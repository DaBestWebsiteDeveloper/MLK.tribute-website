A Timeline of Martin Luther King's Life:

Part 1 - Early Life

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia
  • 1944 - Attends the all-black Morehouse College
  • 1948 - Left Morehouse College with a Bachelor's Degree in sociology
  • 1951 - Entered Boston University to study for a PhD which he would get in 1955
  • 1953 - Married the love of his life, Coretta Scott King

Part 2 - The Civil Rights Movement

  • 1955 - Began his debut in the Civil Rights Movement by leading the Montgomery Bus Boycott
  • 1957 - Became the leader of the SCLC (Southern Christian Leadership Conference) which helped organise non-violent protests
  • 1959 - Took inspiration from Ghandi's non-violent protest stance during his visit to India
  • April-May 1963 - Birmingham Marches took place and MLK (Martin Luther King) was among those arrested, later writing his famous 'Letter from Birmingham Jail' whilst in prison
  • August 1963 - MLK's famous 'I have a dream speech' took place at his March on Washington

Part 3 - Final Years

  • 1964 - Helps pass the Civil Rights Act and is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1965 - Leads the brutal Selma to Montgomery march which lead to the passing of the Voting Rights Act
  • 1968 - Assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee while supporting black sanitary workers on strike
  • 1983 - MLK's birthday becomes recognised as a federal holiday

Final messages

"The ultimate measure of a man is not where he stands in moments of comfort and convenience, but where he stands at times of challenge and controversy." - Martin Luther King
